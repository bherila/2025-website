---
title: Ultra-Crispy Spicy-Sweet Fried Chicken
categories:
  - Meat
ingredients:
  - 1 kg / 2.2 lb chicken wings, drumettes, flats, or small drumsticks
  - 42 g / about 1/4 cup plus 1 teaspoon Seah's-style spicy fried chicken spice mix
  - 1 egg white, or 15 ml / 1 tablespoon cold water
  - 30 g / about 1/4 cup potato starch or cornstarch
  - 15 g / about 1 1/2 tablespoons rice flour
  - 2 g / 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  - Neutral frying oil, enough for 5-8 cm / 2-3 inches depth
  - 4 g / 1 teaspoon powdered sugar, optional
  - 1 g / 1/2 teaspoon chili powder, optional
  - 0.3 g / 1/16 teaspoon salt, optional
  - 0.3 g / 1/16 teaspoon MSG, optional
---

Dry the chicken well. Pat it dry with paper towels, then toss it with the egg white or water and about 30 g / 3 tablespoons of the spice mix. Massage until the chicken is sticky and evenly coated.

Rest at least 30 minutes. For better texture and seasoning, refrigerate uncovered for 2 to 12 hours.

Make the crisping dredge by mixing the remaining 12 g / about 1 tablespoon plus 1 teaspoon spice mix with the potato starch or cornstarch, rice flour, and baking powder.

Toss the marinated chicken in the dredge and press it on firmly. Set the coated chicken on a rack for 10 to 15 minutes, until the surface looks slightly damp and shaggy.

Fry in batches at 325-335°F / 163-168°C until pale golden and mostly cooked through, about 6 to 8 minutes for wings or 10 to 14 minutes for drumsticks, depending on size.

Rest the chicken on a rack for 10 minutes. Increase the oil to 375°F / 190°C and fry again for 60 to 120 seconds, just until deeply crisp.

Use a thermometer to confirm the chicken reaches 165°F / 74°C internally.

For a snack-seasoning finish, stir together the powdered sugar, chili powder, salt, and MSG. Sprinkle a little over the chicken while it is still hot.

For a closer packet-style version, skip the extra starch dredge and use the full 42 g / about 1/4 cup plus 1 teaspoon spice mix directly on 1 kg / 2.2 lb chicken. For the crunchier version, keep the starch and baking powder step.
