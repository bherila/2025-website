---
title: Ultra-Crispy Spicy-Sweet Fried Chicken
categories:
  - Meat
ingredients:
  - 2.2 lb / 1 kg chicken wings, drumettes, flats, or small drumsticks
  - 1 batch Seah's-style spicy fried chicken spice mix
  - 1 egg white, or 1 tablespoon cold water
  - 30 g potato starch or cornstarch
  - 15 g rice flour
  - 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  - Neutral frying oil
  - 1 teaspoon powdered sugar, optional
  - 1/2 teaspoon chili powder, optional
  - Pinch of salt, optional
  - Pinch of MSG, optional
---

Dry the chicken well. Pat it dry with paper towels, then toss it with the egg white or water and about 30 g of the spice mix. Massage until the chicken is sticky and evenly coated.

Rest at least 30 minutes. For better texture and seasoning, refrigerate uncovered for 2 to 12 hours.

Make the crisping dredge by mixing the remaining 12 g spice mix with the potato starch or cornstarch, rice flour, and baking powder.

Toss the marinated chicken in the dredge and press it on firmly. Set the coated chicken on a rack for 10 to 15 minutes, until the surface looks slightly damp and shaggy.

Fry in batches at 325-335 F until pale golden and mostly cooked through, about 6 to 8 minutes for wings or 10 to 14 minutes for drumsticks, depending on size.

Rest the chicken on a rack for 10 minutes. Increase the oil to 375 F and fry again for 60 to 120 seconds, just until deeply crisp.

Use a thermometer to confirm the chicken reaches 165 F internally.

For a snack-seasoning finish, stir together the powdered sugar, chili powder, salt, and MSG. Sprinkle a little over the chicken while it is still hot.

For a closer packet-style version, skip the extra starch dredge and use the full 42 g spice mix directly on 1 kg chicken. For the crunchier version, keep the starch and baking powder step.
