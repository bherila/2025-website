---
title: Seah's-Style Spicy Fried Chicken Spice Mix
categories:
  - Meat
ingredients:
  - 8 g / about 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  - 6 g / about 2 teaspoons fine rice flour
  - 4 g / about 2 teaspoons mild red chili powder, gochugaru, or Kashmiri chili
  - 2 g / about 3/4 teaspoon cayenne or Thai chili powder
  - 3 g / about 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  - 1.25 g / about 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  - 1.25 g / about 1/2 teaspoon ground white pepper
  - 9 g / about 2 teaspoons superfine sugar
  - 6 g / about 1 teaspoon fine salt
  - 1.3 g / about 1/3 teaspoon MSG
  - 0.2 g / scant 1/16 teaspoon I+G powder, optional
---

Makes one 42 g / about 1/4 cup plus 1 teaspoon packet equivalent, enough for about 1 kg / 2.2 lb chicken wings, drumettes, or small drumsticks.

Mix all ingredients very thoroughly. For the most even packet-style texture, pulse the mixture a few times in a spice grinder so the sugar, salt, flours, and spices become one uniform powder.

The MSG and tiny pinch of I+G powder give the mix its commercial seasoning-packet savoriness. If you do not have I+G, use a little extra MSG or omit it.

For a simple packet-style chicken, rub the full batch onto 1 kg / 2.2 lb chicken wings, marinate for 30 minutes, then deep-fry until crisp and cooked through.

This is a Seah's-style copycat, not the official product. It is based on the commercial spicy packet's published ingredient list and nutrition panel.
